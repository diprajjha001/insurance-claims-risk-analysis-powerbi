# 📊 Insurance Risk & Claim Analysis Dashboard

An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze insurance policies, claims, and customer demographics to uncover risk patterns and enable data-driven decision-making.

---

## 🚀 Project Overview

This project focuses on exploring insurance data to identify trends in claims, customer behavior, and policy distribution. The dashboard helps stakeholders understand risk exposure and optimize business strategies.

---

## 📌 Key Metrics

- **Total Policies:** 37,542  
- **Total Claim Amount:** $187.8M  
- **Average Claim Frequency:** 0.5  
- **Average Claim Amount:** $5K  

---

## ⚡ Features

### 📍 Policy Overview
- High-level KPIs for quick insights into overall performance  

### 👥 Demographic Analysis
- Gender-wise and age-group-wise policy distribution  
- Identify high-risk customer segments  

### 🚗 Vehicle Insights
- Policies by car make and car usage (Commercial vs Private)  
- Trend analysis based on car manufacturing year  

### 🌍 Coverage Analysis
- Distribution across Urban, Suburban, and Rural zones  

### 🎓 Education & Claim Behavior
- Claim amount comparison across education levels and marital status  

---

## 🎨 Dashboard Highlights

- Clean and modern dark-themed UI  
- Interactive filters and slicers  
- Combination of bar charts, donut charts, and trend visuals  
- Easy-to-navigate layout for better storytelling  

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- **Power BI** – Data visualization and dashboard creation  
- **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – Calculations and measures  
- **Data Modeling** – Relationship building and optimization  

---

## 📂 Dataset

The dataset includes information related to:

- Insurance policies  
- Claim amounts and frequency  
- Customer demographics (age, gender, marital status, education)  
- Vehicle details  
- Geographic coverage zones  

> ⚠️ Note: Dataset used for learning and demonstration purposes.

---

## 🎯 Project Objective

To build an interactive analytics dashboard that helps identify claim patterns, assess risk factors, and support better decision-making in the insurance domain.
